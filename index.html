What the analysis found — and what I would do about it

1
The payer outlier. BlueShield Co denies 20.7% of claims — nearly double the 14.4% book average — and pays in 58 days vs 29 for the best payer. When one payer is both the worst denier and the slowest payer, that is a payer-specific problem. Fix: escalate via payer relations and add payer-specific claim-scrubbing rules before submission — not more AR calling, which treats the symptom monthly.
2
The Q4 prior-auth spike. Authorization denials jumped ~60% in Oct–Dec (47, 50, 56 vs a ~30 baseline) and then dropped back — the signature of a payer adding procedures to its prior-auth list while the front desk worked an outdated checklist. Fix: a front-end checklist update for the affected CPT codes. Auth denials recover only ~20% of the time, so each one prevented is worth roughly five worked.
3
The AR concentration. 82% of open AR ($504K of $616K) sits past 90 days, dominated by a small number of high-value claims. Fix: work the AR queue ranked by dollar value × age rather than first-in-first-out — fifty correctly-chosen claims recover more than five hundred random ones.
